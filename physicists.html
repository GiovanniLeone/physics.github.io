Albert Einstien

Albert Einstein(1879-1955) was a German physicist who developed the theory of relativity. He also explained the photoelectric effect which won him the nobel prize. Much of the technology we use today sych as atomic energy is the result of Einstien. He then later developed the equation E = mc(c). In 1931, Einstien was targeted by the Nazis and was forced to migrate to the United States. He is considered the most influential physicist of the 20th century.

Marie Curie

Marie Curie (1867-1934) is the first woman to win a nobel prize in 1903 for discovering radioactivity. She then won her second nobel prize in 1911. In 1934, Curie is killed in a road accident

Niels Bohr

Niels Bohr(1885-1962), born in Copernhagh, developed the mordern structure of the atom. Which has a nucleus at the center with electrons revolving around it. Because of this new idea, Bohr won the Nobel Prize in 1992. In 1954, eight year before his death, Bohr helped establish the European particle physics facility called Cern.

Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking(1942-2018) has defied all emotional, physical and academic boundaries. He suffered from a disease called amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, a disease that paralyized him. He defied the doctor's prediction of a two year life expectancy in 1963. Despite Hawking's disadvantages, he still dedicated his life's work to academia and pubilshed A Brief History Of Time. His book that discusses his theories of cosmology.
